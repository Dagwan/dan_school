What do BISW tuition fees cover?

Teaching
  • Classroom instruction, supervision, and in-school workshops
Meal Plan
  • Pre-Nursery through Year 11 enrollment packages include the Meal Plan.
  • Enrollment packages for Years 12 and 13 do not include the Meal Plan, BUT parents and guardians can opt-in their child. Contact the Finance Department for details.
  • Lunches include an entree, two sides, a dessert, and a drink. Learn more here.
Assessment Costs
  • IB Diploma examinations, IGCSEs, Cognitive Ability Tests, etc.
Supplies and Resources
  • Exercise books; the loan of textbooks, supplies, educational materials, and documentation; 1-1 access to iPads for Years 36 in-school and 1-1 tablet or laptop access in-school for Years 7-13; subscriptions and educational memberships; the yearbook; PE T-shirts/shorts; and school photos
Trips
  • Exercise books; the loan of textbooks, supplies, educational materials, and documentation; 1-1 access to iPads for Years 36 in-school and 1-1 tablet or laptop access in-school for Years 7-13; subscriptions and educational memberships; the yearbook; PE T-shirts/shorts; and school photos

2023-24 Registration and Enrollment Fees

Application Fee — $190
  • This is a non-refundable fee.
Enrollment Deposit — $2,000
  • This deposit is due each year to secure enrollment or re-enrollment.
  • It becomes an advance tuition payment subtracted from a later tuition payment.
New Enrollment Fee — $2,500
  • This one-time fee contributes to important capital enhancements at the British International School of Washington.
  • It is non-refundable and in addition to the tuition schedule
  • This fee is due only before the first year of enrollment only.
Refundable Security Deposit — $1,000
  • This fee covers any loss or damage
  • It is due before the first year of enrollment only.

Additional Fees

Potential Obligated Supplementary Fees
  • Learning Support, exceeding 3 hours per week of provision: $30 per hour
Required Supplementary Costs
  • School uniform
Optional Supplementary Costs
  • Additional overnight/multi-day field trips (e.g., MIT visit, Juilliard camps, Global Games, Primary Camps, World Scholars Cup, ski trip, or overnight sports tournaments), on a trip-by-trip basis
  • IB and IGCSE retakes, SAT, SAT II Subject Tests, ACT, or other non-compulsory assessments
  • School bus transportation (Contact the Transportation Department for details.)
  • Childcare (after-school and vacation programs)
  • Externally run clubs, individual music lessons, other language courses, or individual private tutoring
