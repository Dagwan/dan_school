OUTSTANDING EXPERIENCES

DISCOVERY AHEAD

A huge world awaits your child, filled with adventures and opportunities!

CREATING INDIVIDUAL GOALS

We encourage your child to reach for the stars. Our British heritage education curriculum is designed to support their individual abilities and goals, so that our teachers can create exceptional experiences that balance your child’s top academic outcomes with their character development and wellbeing.

Our lively connections with UNICEF and the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals, as well as our collaborations with world-leading institutions like MIT and Julliard, allow our students to do and be their best in a world where they can make a difference.

In turn, our students’ excellent results help them to build their resilience for life and create the pathways to their first-choice universities.

BROADENING HORIZONS

Our exciting global projects are realised through collaborations with world-leading institutions such as Juilliard, UNICEF, and MIT – providing your child with the best resources to develop their natural talents and important life skills.

Our collaboration with the world’s most renowned arts institution enriches our students’ love of dance, drama and music.

The Juilliard School

Performing arts are so much more than what is seen on stage. They encourage self-expression and self-awareness, as well the development of empathy, confidence and cultural exploration. Our collaboration with Juilliard, the world-renowned performing arts school, combined with our outstanding dance, drama, and music programmes, inspires our students’ creativity, team-work and resilience.

The Juilliard School is recognised around the world as a leader in the performing arts; our exclusive collaboration with them harnesses their expertise and experience to create an arts programme that both inspires our students and sparks their passion to pursue their talents and interests.

MIT

The MIT-Dan STEAM programme helps our students to develop their creativity and problem-solving skills by tackling interdisciplinary challenges facing the world today.

×

MIT

Our global programme inspires students to look beyond individual subjects and harness the benefits of an interdisciplinary, practical, hands-on approach to learning guided by the world’s leading STEAM institution. Our students are encouraged to solve problems creatively, to embrace curiosity and to learn from world-leading experts at MIT. It also gives our teachers the chance to learn and receive professional development from inspiring figures in the world of STEAM and pass on their passion for learning to our students.

UNICEF

Students are challenged to make a difference locally and globally by tackling the Sustainable Development Goals.

×

UNICEF

We want to give both our students and our teachers the chance to make a meaningful, positive impact in their community. This includes embedding the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) in all aspects of teaching and learning across our schools, using materials developed by UNICEF.

    Residential Trips

    We offer extended overnight trips to enhance the physical, emotional, and social development of the students. For instance, Year 2 pupils have stayed overnight in Delaware to enrich their unit of work “We’re Going on Holiday”. Years 7, 8, and 9 have visited the premiere educational North Bay Adventure Camp in Maryland, while Years 10 and 11 have taken trips to Camp Horizons in the heart of the Blue Ridge Mountains in Virginia. These experiences always present exciting new challenges that inspire the students in a natural environment.

    Global Classroom

    The Global Classroom is an innovative and modern online educational program unique to Dan Education. It runs school challenges where students receive access to world-leading experts to develop their team building and creative skills in an authentic context. Similarly, the Global Classroom Annual Expedition to Tanzania brings students from around the world together for a face to face experience whilst having an impact on the lives of others.

GOING BEYOND THE CLASSROOM

BISW students have unique opportunities to explore the world and make a difference. Our transformative local, national, and international trips broaden students' horizons and foster lifelong friendships.

Abuja - FCT

Inspiration in the District

Here in the nation's capital, we have world-class museums, historical monuments, and national parks at our doorstep. We take field trips that complement and support in-class lessons and ensure students benefit from our proximity to DC's impressive sites.

Service Trips

Fostering Growth while Helping Others

BISW students take a hands-on approach to service and community support. Local service trips to DC-area charitable organizations help students see the positive impact of their actions right here in Washington, DC. Similarly, service trips abroad help BISW students connect with and aid international communities.

Events through NAE Partnerships

United by NAE

Through NAE's partnerships, BISW students have access to out-of-town activities that unite them with fellow NAE students and the global community. For example, students have visited the UNICEF headquarters in New York; given music, dance, and drama performances at the annual NAE Performing Arts Festival in front of Juilliard-affiliated artists etc.

Camp Stays

Building Confidence and Making Memories at Camp

BISW facilitates sleepaway camp experiences that let students build self-esteem, learn life skills, and work collaboratively. Students return to school with exciting stories about activities they tried, such as horseback riding, team-building exercises, archery, watercraft adventures, canoeing, rock climbing, zip-lining, and more.

Ski Trips

Exciting Winter Sports

During the snowy season, secondary school students learn to ski or snowboard at mountain lodge resorts, such as Mountain Top Lodge Resort. The students stay in cosy executive suites on this annual trip and enjoy activities like snow tubing and indoor swimming.

KEEPING HEALTHY

LOOKING AFTER OUR WELL-BEING

    Your child’s wellbeing is a key part of their personal and academic success. Our attentive staff are available to support your child no matter what – and our dedicated full-time student counsellors work closely with our student community. From secondary school, we also offer regular mindfulness and yoga activities, which are integrated into our physical education curriculum.

    We follow DC Regulations and Nord Anglia Education’s expectations regarding safeguarding and child protection. To keep our students’ minds and bodies fit and flexible, we also use resources from our Global Campus online platform – so that our students are ready to take on new challenges, both in and out of school.

OUR SOCIAL PURPOSE

CARING THAT DRIVES ACTION

As an important part of our curriculum, we challenge our students to go beyond the classroom. Your child will have the opportunity to research, reflect on, plan, and manage their own service projects with communities close to home or far beyond.

Because your child is always at the center of their own learning experiences, we ensure their voice is heard through platforms of positive change including our ambitious Student Council, Global Goals and Model United Nations clubs, which work together to shape the spirit and social direction of our school’s community.

On Global Campus, our Nord Anglia online platform, we also challenge your child through our social impact programme, where our students learn about the UN’s Sustainable Development 2030 agenda and the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.
