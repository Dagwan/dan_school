TAILORED SUPPORT

Our teachers will place your child at the center of their own learning, allowing us to tailor to their specific abilities and interests.

We consider our students’ different learning styles to develop lessons that are integrated across different subjects, based on age-appropriate topics that boost their curiosity.

And while our students’ learning experiences are filled with personal challenge and growth, they lead to fantastic outcomes.

EARLY YEARS

We’ll unlock your child’s potential through our internationally recognized curricula, personalized approach to learning, and integration of subjects. We’ll also guide your child in connecting their questions or interests to the world outside of school.

2 TO 5 YEARS OLD

Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS)

We offer a holistic approach to children's learning through purposeful play, tailoring practical activities and skills to literature and math. We make learning fun through engaging hands-on experiences.

5 TO 11 YEARS OLD

National Curriculum for England (NCE) and the International Primary Curriculum (IPC)

Our Primary curriculum combines the best aspects of the NCE and the IPC. We place each child at the center of their own learning experience, and we develop lessons and activities that offer opportunities for personal challenge, growth, and success.

PRIMARY SCHOOL
(ELEMENTARY)

We build our students’ confidence as they develop new skills.

By exploring modern topics and themes across the subjects, your child will work towards their questions, interests, and goals in various areas of the International Primary Curriculum (IPC).

LOWER SECONDARY
(MIDDLE SCHOOL)

Following the International Middle Years Curriculum and the English National Curriculum, we nurture our lower secondary students’ curiosity so that they love learning. While we have specialist teachers for each subject, we’ll also encourage your child to connect their ideas across the subjects, linking each lesson by “big ideas.”

11 TO 14 YEARS OLD

Learner Profile Diploma and English National Curriculum

At DNPS, our students are introduced to a vast array of new and fascinating ideas designed to develop their critical thinking skills, curiosity, and love of learning. Our lower secondary curriculum is designed to meet the challenges of a rapidly changing world, promoting both academic and personal development.

The International General Certificate of Secondary Education (IGCSE)

Our international program is similar to the UK's General Certificate of Secondary Education (GCSE), although it has more of a global theme.

IGCSE PROGRAMME
(UPPER SECONDARY)

In Years 10 and 11 of High School (Upper Secondary), our students complete the two-year Cambridge IGCSE program, which involves a world-class curriculum that ends in the globally recognized exam and qualification, as developed by Cambridge University Press & Assessment.

Our students go on to achieve impressive IGCSE results, as over half achieve A*–A grades – which is double the global average. And after the IGCSE qualification, they’ll have the ideal preparation for continuing to the impressive International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme (IBDP).

THE IB DIPLOMA PROGRAMME
(UPPER SECONDARY)

From Year 12 onward of High School (Upper Secondary), students at BISW all follow the The International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme to prepare for university. The IB is a challenging two-year course of study that prepares students for success in a global society. This program of study leads to the highly regarded qualification that is widely recognized by the world’s leading universities.

16 TO 18 YEARS OLD

The IB Diploma Programme

The IBDP prepares our students to solve challenges of the future through interdisciplinary study.

They’ll choose three subjects to study at Higher Level (HL) and three to study at Standard Level (SL): Studies in Language and Literature, Language Acquisition, Individuals and Societies, Sciences, Mathematics, and the Arts.

They’ll also have extended essays, service projects, and opportunities to explore specialized knowledge — while taking time to reflect on the nature of knowledge itself!

A TYPICAL DAY AT DNPS

READY FOR THE DAY

Every morning, we gather to greet our classmates, sing our welcome song, and prepare for our exciting day.

EXCITING LESSONS

With a mix of student-led and teacher-led learning, Early Years classes give students a chance to engage with new topics in a fun, stimulating way.

LUNCHTIME

We enjoy delicious, healthy food after a morning of engaging activities. Our snacks and meals are always nutritious and tasty, and this time of day helps us build good table manners and learn to tidy up after ourselves.

THE GREAT OUTDOORS

Being active, playing games, and learning outside are important for children. At BISW, Pre-Nursery and Nursery are steps away from outdoor fun in playgrounds attached to their classrooms, and Reception takes a weekly Walk in the Woods at Dumbarton Oaks Park to explore nature and make discoveries.

EARLY LITERACY

We use books that spark the imagination and encourage us to engage with stories through actions, pictures, and songs. We also provide Phonics lessons through Read Write, Inc. to give students a flying start in learning to read and write.

READY FOR LEARNING

Settling in at registration, we prepare for a full day of learning and get ready for the exciting lessons ahead.

ENGLISH AND MATHS

Our British heritage is evident in our instruction of English and Mathematics lessons that correspond with the English National Curriculum.

LUNCHTIME

In partnership with FLIK Independent School Dining, we offer delicious, healthy, and locally sourced meals for students through our meal service program.

EXPLORING IPC TOPICS

Using the International Primary Curriculum (IPC), we explore fascinating topics each half term and follow a thematic approach to learning that lets curiosity guide us through learning pathways. Together, we unlock inventive ways to explore IPC ideas across multiple subjects, such as Art, History, and Science.

SPECIALIST LESSONS

Each week, students take specialist classes, such as Physical Education, Performing Arts and Spanish, with instructors who provide lessons that help students gain confidence and skills.

AFTER-SCHOOL ACTIVITIES

We offer a variety of after-school activities and clubs that help students explore their interests. Participating in these helps students develop their skills and empowers them to try new things.

FORM GROUPS

We take time to get ready for the day and focus on well-being in our form groups at morning registration. This is a great time to discuss important information like our schedules or details of an upcoming activity or presentation.

EXPLORING BIG IDEAS

The programme in Lower Secondary (Years 7 to 9) lets us explore big ideas through the lens of multiple subjects. We work on areas that makes us curious and connect reference points from various subjects to specific themes or topics.

LUNCHTIME

In partnership with FLIK Independent School Dining, we offer delicious, healthy, and locally sourced meals for students through our meal service program.

A BREADTH OF OPPORTUNITIES

Lower secondary offers a wide range of ways for students to learn. For example, we artistically express ourselves while uncovering and showcasing our talents in Music, Dance and Drama, and we discover the world around us during lab times in science courses like Biology, Chemistry, and Physics.

BEYOND EXTRACURRICULARS

With sports teams in addition to academic and hobby clubs, the range of extracurricular activities at BISW includes something fun for everyone and ensures students can explore their interests. Students have access to field trips to important sites in the DC area. Furthermore, we provide residential trips to overnight camps.

STANDING OUT WITH THE IB

Students in Years 12 and 13 work through the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme (IBDP), a widely acclaimed programme accepted and recognized for entry into higher education and universities worldwide.

BUILDING LEADERSHIP SKILLS

DNPS upper secondary students show leadership skills by serving the community. From helping charitable organizations in the DC area to assisting communities abroad on service trips, there is an abundance of opportunities to help those around us.

INSPIRING YOUR CHILD

HOW WE TEACH

Our internationally experienced teachers can easily connect with your child within our small class sizes, and they’ll always put the interests of your student first.

Our teachers define individualized goals under the English National Curriculum, and they passionately care about the progress your child will make – as they’re always determining the best way forward to ensure your child’s success.

As a result, 92% of our parents enthusiastically believe that we’ve supported their child’s talents.

TEACHING EXCELLENCE

LEARNING FROM THE BEST

DNPS’s outstanding teachers create the very best educational experiences in the world. That’s because over 90% of our teachers are experienced in British heritage education – which means your child is in the best hands with our expert teachers.

They’re also equipped with unique training materials from our award-winning professional development platform, Nord Anglia University, as well as world-class resources from our collaborators, MIT and Juilliard.

We always support our teachers through a regular performance review process, so that we’ll take the time to evaluate and reflect, thoughtfully ensuring that our educational techniques and tools are best-in-class.
